Carnivore Diet Review

The Carnivore Diet is a restrictive diet that involves exclusively consuming animal products and excludes plant-based foods. Followers of this diet generally only eat animal-based foods and avoid all plant-based foods, aiming to get their nutrients solely from meat, fish, and animal products.

a plate of meat

What you can eat

On a carnivore diet, the primary focus is on consuming animal-based foods. Here are the foods typically included in a carnivore diet:

  • Meat: Beef, pork, lamb, veal, game meats, and other red meats are staples in a carnivore diet.
  • Poultry: Chicken, turkey, duck, and other poultry products are commonly consumed.
  • Fish: Various types of fish, including fatty fish like salmon and mackerel, are allowed.
  • Organ Meats: Liver, kidney, heart, and other organ meats are often included for their nutrient content.
  • Eggs: Eggs are a versatile source of protein and healthy fats.
  • Bone Broth: Some individuals include bone broth for its potential health benefits.
  • Animal Fats: Fats from animal sources, such as tallow and lard, are used for cooking and added to meals.
  • Dairy: Some carnivore diets allow full-fat dairy products like butter and hard cheeses, although this may vary.

What you can't eat

The carnivore diet restricts all plant-based foods, including fruits, vegetables, grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, and plant-derived oils. Processed foods, sweeteners, dairy (depending on the diet variation), and beverages like sugary drinks are also excluded. Some versions of the diet allow for limited seasonings, while others may eliminate them entirely. Alcoholic beverages are typically avoided as well.

The Benefits of the Carnivore Diet

Proponents of the carnivore diet claim benefits like improved digestion and weight loss, however, scientific research on its long-term effects and potential risks is limited. Some of the diet's claimed benefits include simplicity, weight loss, improved digestive health, enhanced satiety, and allergen avoidance. Followers often report heightened mental clarity, sustained energy levels, and potential alleviation of symptoms in autoimmune conditions. Additionally, the diet is perceived to help identify specific food sensitivities through its elimination of plant-based foods. Some individuals also claim reduced inflammation as a result of this diet.

Issues with the Carnivore Diet

The carnivore diet comes with many potential disadvantages and risks. Its extreme restriction of plant-based foods can lead to deficiencies in essential nutrients like vitamins, minerals, and fiber. The diet's high saturated fat content may impact cardiovascular health, and the absence of carbohydrates can affect energy levels and brain function for some individuals. The lack of variety may pose challenges in meeting long-term dietary needs. Additionally, the sustainability and social aspects of such a restrictive diet may be difficult to maintain. Overall it is a diet that is not recommended.

Use as part of an overall weight loss strategy

Relying solely on the carnivore diet as a weight loss strategy might overlook the broader aspects of healthy weight management, which should encompass balanced nutrition, regular exercise, proper hydration, and overall well-being. Any diet should be approached with caution and in consultation with a healthcare professional or registered dietitian.
