ACS Scholars Program

Launched in 1994, the ACS Scholars Program provides scholarships as well as mentoring and internship opportunities to talented Black, Indigenous, and Latina/o/x, and Hispanic students enrolled in chemical sciences programs at accredited two- and four-year colleges. The program also engages students in scientific conferences and professional development programming to help them reach their academic and professional goals.

Advancing Chemistry Teaching

To support and empower K-12 educators in their important work, ACS offers a broad portfolio of classroom and web-based resources. These lessons, programs, and activities help teachers spark students’ imagination, highlight the role of chemistry in everyday life, and introduce fundamental concepts and practices.

Project Seed

Established in 1968, Project SEED provides paid summer laboratory experiences to economically disadvantaged high school students across the nation. Through the program, students discover exciting career paths and develop college readiness and professional development skills. Dedicated scientist mentors ensure that Project SEED participants complete the program with an enhanced appreciation for science and a clearer vision for their future.

U.S. National Chemistry Olympiad

Established in 1984, the U.S. National Chemistry Olympiad invites high school students to an annual chemistry competition that tests their knowledge against their peers. Top-scoring students compete for a chance to represent the U.S. at the International Chemistry Olympiad. The program engages many in a rewarding experience that encourages them to further their knowledge of chemistry and shapes their awareness of the global chemistry community.

ACS Bridge Program

The ACS Bridge Project aims to increase the number of Black, Indigenous, Latina/o/x, and Hispanic students earning doctoral degrees in the chemical sciences. In addition to receiving funding for their education, students are matched with academic departments that are establishing new practices to effectively attract and retain underrepresented students, while providing mentoring, research opportunities, and other resources to help them complete their graduate degrees.

Chemical & Engineering News

Since its inception in 1923, Chemical & Engineering News has been a trusted, comprehensive source of chemistry news and information. The magazine’s dedicated staff keeps pace with the current issues and trends of the day and develops compelling features that provide diverse perspectives on the state of the chemistry enterprise.
