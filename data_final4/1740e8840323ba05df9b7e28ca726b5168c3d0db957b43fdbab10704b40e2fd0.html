How to Choose Ski Goggles?

Winter is here and nothing makes us happier than winter snow. Snow comes with so many joyful moments and activities to indulge in. Skiing is one of the most popular activities all over the world. However, just like cricket is incomplete without a bat, skiing is without ski glasses. 

downhill skiing

Whether you are an amateur or a professional in skiing, having the right ski goggles is extremely important. But, wondering what is the right one for you? Well, don’t worry, after an ample amount of research, we have listed how to choose ski goggles. Stay with us till the end and you will get a checklist for your next shopping spree for ski goggles.

What is the Need for Ski Glasses? 

Goggles are worn for a purpose and that is protection. While performing any sport, it is essential to have a clear vision. Many elements like dust, snow, and light can cause a disturbance. Therefore, sports glasses protect our eyes from all these factors while offering a clear vision and enabling us to enjoy the experience to its fullest.

How to Choose Ski Goggles?

Before making a purchase, it is important to do some research so that whatever you pick solves your problem and offers a clear vision. Here is everything you should consider while getting ski glasses.

Check Gender 

Now, most ski goggles come with an adjustable strap so that both women and men can wear them. But, if you are looking for gender-specific ski glasses then make sure it is according to your face size. Women have comparatively smaller faces than men, so make sure the one you are picking is either adjustable or fits your face.

Right Size

Another crucial factor when buying ski goggles is considering the right face size. Some people might have a smaller face whereas some will have a bigger face. So, there are usually three categories which include small, medium and large. One can get the pair according to the dimension and size chart.

Goggle Lenses Types

Now, it is important what type of lenses you are getting in your ski glasses because they are most important when it comes to protecting your eyes. Let’s look at different types of lenses types. 

  • Polarisation: These coatings are important to reduce the glare and light that gets reflected through snow. However, polarised lenses are not a safe choice for skiing as they affect depth perception. It’s best to avoid polarised lenses for ski goggles. 
  • Mirrored Lenses: Mirrored lenses are any day a better choice compared to polarised lenses. They reduce glare around the snow like polarised lenses but don’t interfere with contrast or depth perception.
  • Revo Lenses: Revo lenses are developed by NASA scientists and are the absolute best choice when it comes to ski goggles. They feature the benefits of mirrored lenses and many more.
skiing goggles

Different Colour Lenses

So the lesser-known fact about ski goggles is that the lenses come in various colours and every colour has a reason behind it. Let’s understand what each colour is significant for. 

  • Grey, Blue, Brown: These lenses are perfect for sunny conditions as they offer a VLT of 8-18%.
  • Amber, Gold, Pink: Works perfect for cloudy and foggy conditions, as they come with VLT of 18-43%. 
  • Yellow and Clear: Perfect for nighttime conditions as they have the highest VLT of 80-100%. 

Tips for Ski Goggles

  • If you already have a helmet then check if ski glasses are helmet compatible.
  • It is one of the most important investments you will make. Hence, you must take good care of them. Don’t clean them harshly. 
  • If you feel the lenses get fogged up, then use the anti-fog spray. Doing this will ensure that you have a smooth ski experience. 
  • Check if your glasses have ventilation because if not then you will end up fogging your lenses more often. Anti-fog coating on top of it will make the experience even better.

Ski Glasses Protection & Maintenance

  • Just like your sunglasses get those scratches while transporting them from one place to another, so do ski goggles. Hence, it is advised that you should always keep them inside the protective case or sleeve. 
  • If you want to clean your sports glasses, make sure you do it very gently with the cleaning cloth provided. Do not clean the inside area with any harsh material as it can ruin the anti-fog layer. 
  • Avoid using paper towels, tissues and napkins as they can take off the protective layer from your glasses.
  • Don’t use any chemicals to clean them because rather than cleaning they can actually ruin your ski glasses. 

Wrapping It Up

Performing a sport requires you to have proper gear and so does skiing. You can’t enjoy skiing if your eyes can’t see properly. Hence, it is important that you invest in good ski goggles that not only protect your eyes but also stay with you for long.
