FIFA Club World Cup USA 2025: The Ultimate Guide

The FIFA Club World Cup 2025 introduces a 32-team format. This marks a major expansion from the traditional 7-team tournament. The competition takes place across multiple American cities from June 15 to July 13, 2025.

MetLife Stadium in New Jersey will host the final

This expanded version represents FIFA's vision for international club football. The tournament offers a $1 billion prize pool. The official FIFA Club World Cup website provides regular updates on preparations.

What makes this tournament unique? It brings together champion clubs from every continent in a comprehensive month-long competition to determine a true world club champion.

What Is the FIFA Club World Cup?

The FIFA Club World Cup is an international football competition. It features champion clubs from FIFA's six continental confederations, plus the host nation's representative. The tournament determines the best club team in the world.

The competition began in its modern format in 2000, though the inaugural tournament was canceled. It evolved from the Intercontinental Cup, which ran from 1960 to 2004 between European and South American champions.

The tournament launched officially in 2005 with a 7-team format. European clubs have dominated the competition, winning 15 of the 19 editions to date.

The Evolution of the Tournament

The Club World Cup developed through several distinct phases:

  • 1960-2004: The Intercontinental Cup era (European vs South American champions)
  • 2000: First attempt at an expanded Club World Championship in Brazil
  • 2005-2023: 7-team format with continental champions
  • 2025: Launch of the 32-team format in the USA

This expansion moves the tournament closer to the scale and prestige of the FIFA World Cup and UEFA Champions League.

FIFA Club World Cup 2025 Format

The 2025 FIFA Club World Cup adopts a completely new format. The tournament expands from seven to 32 teams. It uses a structure similar to the FIFA World Cup format from 1998-2022.

The official FIFA breakdown details how matches will be organized throughout the competition.

Group Stage Format

The group stage consists of:

  • 32 teams in 8 groups of 4 teams each
  • 3 matches per team against group opponents
  • 3 points for a win, 1 for a draw, 0 for a loss
  • Top two teams from each group advance
  • Tiebreakers include goal difference and head-to-head results

This creates 96 group stage matches across multiple American venues.

Knockout Stage Format

The knockout phase follows a direct elimination structure:

  • Round of 16: 16 qualified teams
  • Quarter-finals: 8 teams
  • Semi-finals: 4 teams
  • Final: 2 teams

All knockout matches use extra time and penalty shootouts if tied after 90 minutes. The prize money distribution reaches up to $125 million for the winning team.

FIFA Club World Cup Qualification Process

The qualification routes for the 32 teams include:

  • UEFA (12 slots): Champions League winners (2021-2024) plus clubs based on UEFA coefficient ranking
  • CONMEBOL (6 slots): Copa Libertadores winners (2021-2024) plus clubs based on CONMEBOL ranking
  • CONCACAF, AFC, CAF (4 slots each): Continental champions (2021-2024)
  • OFC (1 slot): Highest-ranked club among OFC Champions League winners (2021-2024)
  • Host nation (1 slot): Inter Miami CF (2024 MLS Supporters' Shield winners)
