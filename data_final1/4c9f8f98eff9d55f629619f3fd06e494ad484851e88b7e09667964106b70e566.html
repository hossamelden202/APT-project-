What is Yoga?

Yoga is an ancient practice with a history spanning thousands of years. It is not just a form of physical exercise; it's a holistic approach to achieving balance, both mentally and physically.

Yoga aims to promote harmony and unity within the self, and with the world around us. Yoga encompasses a range of many different techniques, including body postures and movement, breathing exercises, relaxation, concentration and meditation. There are many different styles of yoga, and the intensity and difficulty, and therefore the fitness and health benefits vary between styles.

Here is a list of the diverse yoga styles with a brief description of each.

  • Hatha Yoga: This foundational yoga style focuses on physical postures and alignment, offering a gentle introduction to yoga. It promotes physical fitness, relaxation, and an improved sense of well-being.
  • Vinyasa Yoga: Characterized by fluid and dynamic movements, Vinyasa yoga synchronizes breath with a continuous series of poses. It builds strength, flexibility, endurance, and enhances cardiovascular health. Practicing Vinyasa yoga fosters mindfulness as you flow through movements.
  • Iyengar Yoga: Precision is the hallmark of Iyengar yoga. It uses props like belts and blocks to ensure correct alignment in postures, making it a therapeutic choice for those with physical limitations. Iyengar yoga improves posture, alignment, stability, and overall well-being.
  • Bikram Yoga: Performed in a room heated to high temperatures, Bikram yoga consists of a fixed sequence of 26 poses. The heat enhances flexibility and detoxification, making it an ideal choice for cleansing the body and improving physical fitness.
  • Ashtanga Yoga: This fast-paced and intense practice involves a set sequence of postures, fostering strength and endurance. Ashtanga yoga is a full-body workout that enhances physical fitness, flexibility, and mental focus.
  • Kundalini Yoga: Kundalini yoga aims to awaken the dormant energy at the base of the spine. It incorporates dynamic breathing, mantra chanting, and meditation, promoting emotional balance, self-awareness, and mental clarity.
