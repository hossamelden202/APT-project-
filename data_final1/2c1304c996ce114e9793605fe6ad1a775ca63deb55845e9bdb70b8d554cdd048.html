W is for Wrestling: List of 'W' Sports

Here are all the 'W' sports from over 800 sports listed on our complete list of sports. A popular W sport is Wrestling, less popular are the water sports. We all know Water Polo, but have you heard of Water Basketball and Water Volleyball? I like the idea of Walking Football.

There were too many to describe on just one page, so here we have a short description of just the sports starting with the letter 'W'. Click the sport name for more information.

'W' Sports

  • Wakeboarding Ś riding a board and doing tricks on the wake behind a boat.
  • Walking Football Ś a version of association football in which players are only allowed to walk during game play, developed for older people to keep fit.
  • Walking Netball Ś a variation of netball, in which players can only walk, developed for older people to keep active.
  • Wallball Ś a wall-based sport similar to squash in which a hi-bounce rubber ball is hit with the hand onto a wall after a single bounce.
  • Wallyball Ś a variation of volleyball, that is played on a racquetball court enclosed with walls on all four sides.
  • Washer Pitching Ś a target throwing game similar to horseshoes, in which players as a part of teams take turns to toss washers into a box or hole.
  • Water Basketball Ś a mix of basketball and water polo played in a swimming pool with basketball goals.
  • Water Polo Ś a team sport played in swimming pools, the aim is to pass the ball over the water and into the goal net.
  • Water Skiing Ś riders are pulled along behind a boat skimming atop the water wearing one or two skis.
  • Water Volleyball Ś a team sport derived from volleyball in which the games are played in water.
  • Weightlifting Ś in Olympic Weightlifting competitors attempt to lift weights, mounted on barbells, above their head.
wrestling match
  • Weight Throw Ś a family of heavy weight throwing events, in which the objective is to either throw the weight as far as possible or as high as possible.
  • Western Pleasure Ś a horse show competition in which horses are evaluated for their manners and composure.
  • Wheelchair Basketball Ś basically regular basketball played on a wheelchair.
  • Wheelchair Curling Ś a variation of curling in which athletes with a disability affecting their lower limbs use a wheelchair to play the sport.
  • Wheelchair Dancing Ś all of the participants of a team perform dance routines while riding in a wheelchair.
  • Wheelchair Fencing Ś a version of Fencing in which disabled athletes fight with thin swords while sitting in wheelchairs that are tightly fastened to the floor.
  • Wheelchair Racing Ś a type of racing in which athletes with physical disabilities compete with the help of a wheelchair.
  • Wheelchair Rugby Ś a full-contact indoor team sport conducted for players with disabilities.
  • Wheelchair Rugby League Ś a version of rugby league football but played using a wheelchair.
  • Wheelchair Tennis Ś a version of lawn tennis for those who have lower body disabilities.
  • Whitewater Slalom Ś a kayaking and canoeing sport in which athletes use a kayak or canoe to navigate through a course that consists of hanging gates on river rapids.
  • Wiffle Ball Ś a variation of Baseball designed for indoor or outdoor play in confined areas, using a perforated, light-weight, rubbery plastic ball and a long, plastic yellow bat.
  • Windsurfing Ś riding a modified surfboard manoeuvered using a sail on a movable mast.
  • Wingsuit Flying Ś a type of skydiving, where a person will fly in the air using a special jumpsuit called the wingsuit.
  • Winter Guard Ś an indoor sport of the color guard discipline in which teams perform several routines using supporting equipment to recorded background music.
  • Winter Triathlon Ś athletes compete in running, mountain biking and cross-country skiing.
  • Women's lacrosse Ś a limited-contact version of lacrosse.
  • Woodball Ś a sport where a mallet is used to pass a ball through gates. This game can be played on grass, sand or indoor. See also Beach Woodball.
  • Wood Chopping Ś participants attempt to cut or saw a log or other types of wood in the quickest time.
  • Wrestling Ś a general term for a combat sport between two competitors involving grappling-type techniques. Popular forms include the Olympic styles of Greco-Roman and Freestyle. There are many regional forms of wrestling.
  • Wushu Ś a full-contact sport derived from traditional Chinese martial arts.
