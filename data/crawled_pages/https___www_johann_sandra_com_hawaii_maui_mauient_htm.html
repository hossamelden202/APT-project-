Maui Entertainment & Night Life

MAUI GUIDEBOOKS
 FROM THE HAWAII BOOKSTORE
 The web's largest selection of Maui Books

Night life on Maui can be hard to come by, compared to the touristy places in other areas, such as Waikiki or Mexico.  The island is quite a calm retreat for the most part, and what night life exists is primarily in Lahaina.  We stayed in Kihei, where there was just nothing happening.  A couple of restaurants around had live music a couple of nights a week, but there were no night clubs or the like.   

One mainstay of Hawaiian night life is the Luau.  Unlimited food and drink along with two  to three hours of Polynesian music and dancing.  We attended the Old Lahaina Luau, called the best and most authentic luau on Maui,  for the tune of $70pp.  By the time you gorged yourself, got sloshed, and enjoyed 2 1/2 hours of Polynesian dancing, it didn't seem as expensive.  We got there too late for our photo to be taken with the dancers, unfortunately, as we had just flown back in from Molokai, and had to pick up our Advanced Open Water scuba diving cards in Kihei before racing up the coast to Lahaina.  The food was excellent (the poi wasn't as bad as they said), and the drinks kept coming.  The dancing was enjoyable, of course, and we captured a few bootleg video segments to give you an idea of what the dancing is like.

Before ordering any tickets to the Luaus on Maui, check with the brokers on our Discounts & Bargains page to get discounted tickets.  Maui Tours has discounts on the Old Lahaina Luau, Royal Lahaina Luau, and the Feast at Lele.  Keep scrolling down the page to find other sources of information about night life and entertainment on Maui.
