Welcome to the web site for biographies of women in mathematics. These pages are part of a project at Agnes Scott College in Atlanta, Georgia, to illustrate the numerous achievements of women in the field of mathematics. Here you can find biographical essays or comments on the women mathematicians profiled on this site, as well as additional resources about women in mathematics. Did you know? (See the Archive for past announcements)

  • Dusa McDuff, the Joan Lyttle Birman ’48 Professor of Mathematics at Barnard College, will receive the 2025 AMS Leroy P. Steele Prize for Lifetime Achievement at the Joint Mathematics Meeting in Seattle in January, 2025. She is honored "for foundational and far-reaching contributions in C*-algebras and symplectic geometry and topology and for long-continued leadership and mentoring in mathematics". For more information, see the AMS press release.
     
  • Jessica Wan placed 5th among 609 participants (and second among the USA six person team) in the 65th International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO), held this year in Bath, England. The United States team earned First Place in this year's competition. Jessica is the first young woman on the USA IMO team since Sherry Gong was on the team in 2005 and 2007.
     
  • Lai-Sang Young was awarded the 2024 Rolf Schock Prize in Mathematics "for long-lasting and deep contribution to the theory of non-uniformly hyperbolic dynamical systems," according to the prize citation.
     
  • The four members of the USA team earned 1st place in the 2024 European Girls’ Mathematical Olympiad (EGMO) held in Tskaltubo, Georgia from April 11 to 17. In addition, each of them also earned a gold medal for their individual performances, including Hannah Fox who earned 41 of 42 possible points. For more information about EGMO2024, see https://egmo2024.ge/index.html, and to see the problems and scoreboard, see https://www.egmo.org/egmos/egmo13/.
     
  • Claire Voisin, Institut de Mathématiques de Jussieu, France, received the 2024 Crafoord Prize in Mathematics "for outstanding contributions to complex and algebraic geometry, including Hodge theory, algebraic cycles, and hyperk?hler geometry." Voisin is the first woman to receive the Crafoord Prize in Mathematics. For more information, see the news article from the American Mathematical Society, or watch the video from the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences that describes Voisin's work that led to the Crafoord Prize.
     

AWM Biographies Contest

To increase awareness of women's ongoing contributions to the mathematical sciences, the Association for Women in Mathematics sponsors an essay contest for biographies of contemporary women mathematicians and statisticians in academic, industrial, and government careers. This contest is open to students in the following categories: Grades 6-8, Grades 9-12, and College Undergraduate. At least one winning submission will be chosen from each category. Winners will receive a prize, and their essays will be published online at the AWM website. Additionally, a grand prize winner will have his or her submission published in the AWM Newsletter. For more information and to see the results of past Essay Contests, go to https://awm-math.org/awards/student-essay-contest/.
