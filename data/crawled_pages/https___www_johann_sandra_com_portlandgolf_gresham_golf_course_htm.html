GRESHAM GOLF COURSE
GRESHAM, OR

 

Gresham Golf Course is, aptly enough, located in the Portland suburb of Gresham, Oregon.  It is an older course, showing it's age, and features fairly average golf conditions.  We've played this course a few times, but it's pretty ordinary.  There are a couple of holes that you have to hit over water, which are fun, but most of the rest of the course is pretty flat and open.  There are homes lining most of the course, where you'll find yourself bouncing balls off of rooftops if you mis-hit.

GRESHAM GOLF COURSE - OUR COMMENTS     

Maintenance:  This course is showing it's age - the grass is dry and thin, and brown spots are prevalent. 

Scenery: Most straight, flat, open fairways.  The course is surrounded by subdivisions without foliage barriers.  There is a creek that runs through the middle which you have to hit over on a few holes - that's nice.  Otherwise, pretty dull surroundings.

Fun: A few holes where you have to hit over the creek add some fun to the round, but other than that - the course is pretty bland.  Flat, straight, and open.  The third hole has a lake that stretches from the tee to the green - it's only a par-3, but kind of a fun hole.

Safety: You may dodge some balls as you play, but there are no streets to cross.

Staff:  Nice guys here.  They've been very friendly to us.  They'll let you play the back 9 if you ask.

Design: Pretty average - no real man-made hazards to speak of except the lake, but the walks are moderate and the course layout makes sense.

Value:  There are a lot better courses in the area that you can play at for about the same price. 

Other Comments:  The 9th hole is backed by an apartment building - don't overshoot the green or you'll lose your ball over the fence.  The fairways are lined often with homes - if you mis-hit, you'll find your balls bouncing off of rooftops.  We probably won't play this course again - there are just too many better courses for this price range around.
