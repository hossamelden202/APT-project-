History of Chemistry links

  • For those who want a quick overview, Brooks-Cole has mounted a nifty hypertext timeline called "The Story of Chemistry". They have the same taste in art as I do.

  • Carmen Giunta's This Week in the History of Chemistry.

  • The CHEM-HIST E-mail list is devoted to the history of chemistry.

  • Horus Gets In Gear is a collection of links for historians. It includes links on the history of science and technology.

  • The ACS Division of the History of Chemistry publishes the Bulletin for the History of Chemistry.

  • The definitive website on alchemy.
  • Present-day resources (books, web sites, etc, etc) on alchemy.
  • This website allows download of The Alchemy Key, 7th Ed. as a series of PDF files. While I haven't read the text, it is a history of alchemy and discusses much alchemical mysticism.

  • Isis, the quarterly peer-reviewed journal of the History of Science Society. The Society also publishes an annual journal, Osiris, which is devoted to the history of science and its cultural influences.

  • Ambix, the peer-reviewed journal of the Society for the History of Alchemy and Chemistry.

  • The Chemical Heritage Foundation, which publishes Chemical Heritage. Individual subscriptions are free to residents of the United States.

  • Carmen Giunta's history of chemistry bookmarks at Lemoyne University. These include copies of classic papers in chemistry. Another classic papers page is maintained by The Chem Team.

  • Bob Jacobs' History of Chemistry site is just a collection of links, but what a collection...

Philosophy of Chemistry links

  • Jeffry L. Ramsey presents a fine summary of the state of the art in philosophy of chemistry, Perspectives on Science 6.4 (1998) 409-427.

  • HYLE, an international journal of the philosophy of chemistry.

  • Foundations of Chemistry is a second journal devoted to philosophy of chemistry.

  • The International Society for the Philosophy of Chemistry sponsors the Philosophy of Chemistry Discussion List.

  • Smith College now has a pretty decent page on the Philosophy of Chemistry, including a list of general issues.

  • Bob Jacobs' Philosophy of Chemistry site is just a collection of links, but what a collection...
