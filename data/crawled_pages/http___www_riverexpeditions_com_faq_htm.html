Frequently Asked Questions

What Is Whitewater Rafting?

Whitewater rafting is a sport whose participants journey by inflatable boat down a river valley. Along the way, rapids are encountered. The raft rises over tall waves and plunges down steep drops. Paddlers pull as a team to steer the raft clear of boulders, logs, bridge abutments, and dangerous hydraulics.

Is It Safe?

Whitewater rafting is justly famous for adrenaline-pumping action. Less well-known is this fact: Whitewater rafting with a conscientious, professional outfitter is the safest adventure travel sport of all. We at North Cascades River Expeditions are completely dedicated and committed to your safety. Our perfect safety record spans 31 years of professional operation and over 45,000 paying passengers. We’re proud of this record and we pledge our very best to preserve it.

Who Can Go Whitewater Rafting?

Just about anyone over 10 can go whitewater rafting. Bring the whole family. We enjoy teaching rafting fundamentals and most people catch on very quickly.

Why Go Whitewater Rafting?

River running is good, clean fun. A river journey holds adventure, discovery, excitement, challenge, and camaraderie. You even get a little exercise.

Can I bring my camera?

Don't bring your good camera. Pick up a waterproof version of a throwaway model. These cameras are ideal for throwing away. They take great pictures, too.

Should I bring anything?

Just bring dry clothes and a towel and leave them in your car. Do not bring luggage containers. Extra sweaters, gloves, or paddling jackets can be carried in our dry bags. So can personal medical supplies that people need to bring.
