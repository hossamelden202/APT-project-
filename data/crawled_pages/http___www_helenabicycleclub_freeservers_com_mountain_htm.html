Let's hit the trail!  The Helena Bicycle Club organizes several mountain bike rides during the season.  The level of difficulty varies from gravel roads to technical single track, flat to mountainous terrain.  Take a look at the schedule... we're sure you'll find something that fits your ability.
Date/Time Ride/Event For Additional Information
Monday Nights
All Summer		 Monday Night Mountain Bike Rides
Relaxed pace, two-hour mountain bike rides on local mountain bike trails around Helena.  Rides leave from the patio of the Lewis & Clark Library at 6:30 p.m.  Great rides to help improve your mountain biking skills.

May 14 through July 30... leave at 6:30 p.m.
August 6 through 27... leave at 6 p.m.

June leader: David Fulkerson 406-442-7852

July Leader: David Thompson 406-443-3166
June 16, Saturday Scratch Gravels Mountain Bike Ride
Join club president, Mark Reinsel, as he leads us along his favorite trails in the Scratch Gravels.  Meet at the Reinsel residence, 1030 Vallejo, at 9 a.m. for this 13-mile ride.   Mark describes the ride as fun with a 1,000-foot elevation gain and a few technical areas.		 Mark Reinsel 406-458-8901
June 23, Saturday Priest Pass Mountain Bike Ride
Mountain bike from the backside of Great Divide Ski area over Priest Pass.  Approximately 30 miles.  Meet at Fireside Coffee, 1446 Euclid, at 9 a.m. to carpool to trailhead.		 Susan Brown 406-442-2782
July 7, Saturday Hiawatha Route - The Tunnels Ride
MEMBERS ONLY RIDE... This is a ride for the whole family!  Bicycle through nine tunnels and over seven former railroad trestles on this scenic Rails-to-Trails ride on the Montana-Idaho border.		 Susan Brown 406-442-2782
August 4, Saturday Willard Creek  Mountain Bike Ride
12-mile loop.  Rolling terrain with combination of Forest Service roads and singletrack.  Meet at Hugo's Pizza in Montana City at 8 a.m.		 Bill Ramsay 406-227-6036
August 11, Saturday Minnehaha Loop Mountain Bike Ride
This 16-mile loop is the mountain biker's mini double divide ride.  A combination of Forest Service roads, doubletrack and singletrack.  Meet at Moose Creek Campground (about 4 miles down road to Rimini) at 8 a.m.

Bring your camping gear and stay the night... many other mountain biking trails leave from the same campground.

 Doug Brown 406-442-2782

Anne Wolfinger 406-449-6488
Sept 1-3, Saturday-Monday Kings Hill Cycling Weekend
MEMBERS ONLY RIDE... Bring the whole family, both cyclists and non-cyclists, to Kings Hill Scenic Byway for a weekend of biking, hiking, camping, and socializing.		 David and Karen  Thompson 406-443-3166
September 8, Saturday Blackfoot Meadows
The perfect mountain bike ride for a fall day... approximately 12 miles in length.  Meeting time and location yet to be determined.		 Russ Cater 40-443-5197
