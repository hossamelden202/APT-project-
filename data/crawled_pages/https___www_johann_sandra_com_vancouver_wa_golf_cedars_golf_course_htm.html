THE CEDARS GOLF COURSE
BRUSH PRAIRIE, WA

 

The Cedars on Salmon Creek just north of Vancouver is one of our favorite area golf courses.  It's kind of in the boonies, so if you're from Portland it might be a stretch, but it's a fun course with varied scenery winding in and out of the cedar trees, crossing bridge after bridge over Salmon Creek, with many holes that require actual strategy instead of just drive-pitch-putt-putt in a straight line.  Dog legs, creeks, and hills make this course a lot of fun.

THE CEDARS GOLF COURSE - OUR COMMENTS

Maintenance:  The Cedars does feature some evidence of subpar maintenance - there are brown, thin spots on the fairways, and on the tee boxes.  Many of the fairways look like they could really use some overseeding and definitely a new irrigation system.  The course just underwent a management change, so we'll see if this is addressed.

Scenery: The course is heavily treed - mostly Cedars of course - and winds in and around Salmon Creek, Duck Ponds, with many bridges.  There are a few spots where the course is bordered by nice homes, but only a few - mostly it's isolated.  There are wooded areas followed by open areas, all with geese, ducks, and Great Blue Herons watching on.  Quite pleasant and relaxing.

Fun: This is perhaps our favorite course we've played in the area - the many creeks and water hazards keep it interesting - you have to actually strategize quite a lot as to where to lay up and where to drive.  The 9th hole is especially interesting - it crosses a creek, dog-legs just before a marsh, crosses the creek again, then immediately launches upward to the green, probably a good 100 feet above.  You have a smile on your face when you leave.

Safety: The course is designed well, so that the fairways are not facing each other, you don't cross any streets to speak of, and you don't have to worry about being hit by other people's stray shots.  Should be an accident-free day.

Staff:  The staff has always been courteous to us so far - haven't talked with them much yet, but so far so good.  I did ask about golf gloves, but the lady at the counter wasn't too much help...the pro shop was almost out of gloves, and we have yet to go there when they have real scorecards - they are using photocopies.

Design: A well-designed course.  The grounds are nice and peaceful, the natural wetlands are a nice touch.  The fairways aren't facing each other - most of the course you can't even see the other golfers.  The walks between greens are moderate and scenic.  Some paths are asphalt - others are gravel. 

Value:  This is a professional-length course that is very fun to play where you can get a round for just $20 in off-season, or with one of their coupons from the Entertainment Book or the Golf Card.  We feel like we got our money's worth out of this course each time we play.

Other Comments:  Due to the extensive water on this course, expect to lose several balls during a day if you're not that good.  Come prepared.
