Victoria Museums

As the capital city of British Columbia, Victoria is home to many excellent museums.  For more historical information, visit Victoria Historical Buildings.

For up to 50% savings on museums such as the Maritime Museum of British Columbia and the Art Gallery of Greater Victoria, grab an Entertainment Book before you go!

Royal British Columbia Museum

Canada's most-visited museum, and easily one of North America's best, you'll find legends, traditions, native american artifacts, mammoths, early settler remnants, an IMAZ theater, and much more.

Art Gallery of Greater Victoria

Housed in the 1889 Spencer Mansion, over 15,000 works of art grace the walls of one of the finest galleries in Canada.  The gallery contains Canada's finest collection of Japanese art, a range of contemporary art, Emily Carr pieces, and traveling exhibits.

Maritime Museum of British Columbia

At the east end of Bastion Square, in the 1889 old provincial courthouse, this museum traces the history of seafaring exploration, adventure, commercial ventures, and  passenger travel. 

British Columbia Aviation Museum

Located near the Victoria International Airport, this museum houses aircraft and artifacts from the aviation industry.

CFB Esquimalt Naval & Military Museum

Located within CFB Esquimalt, this small museum displays naval, military, and general maritime memorabilia.

Centre of the Universe

A 1.8 meter telescope, star parties, interactive exhibits and amazing views of Victoria and the cosmos await visitors to the Dominion Astrophysical Observatory.

Mineral World & Scratch Patch

Visitors to Mineral World can buy gemstones, learn about minerals in interactive exhibits, and find fossils and other artifacts.

Pacific Geoscience Centre

This centre of seismology and marine ecology gives 90 minute tours on weekdays.

Saanich Historical Artifacts Society

One of Western Canada's largest collections of working steam engines, tractors, agricultural machinery, household and industrial artifacts.

Victoria Bug Zoo

Home to a large variety of live insects, arachnids, and other creepy-crawly critters.

Maltwood Art Museum & Gallery

Located at the University of Victoria, the Maltwood Gallery houses a large and varied collection, varying from ancient Chinese artifacts to well-known contemporary pieces.

Royal London Wax Museum

Completed in 1924, this museum features around 300 wax figures direct from London.

Canadian Scottish Regiment Museum

The museum's displays include material from the earliest days of the Regiment to participation in United Nations peacekeeping operations.

Saanich Pioneers Society Museum and Archives

The oldest purpose-built museum in British Columbia, you'll find artifacts from the pioneer days of B.C.

Historical Sydney Museum

This museum houses a marine mammal gallery (dedicated to whales) and a historical gallery.

Sooke Regional Museum

Artifacts and cultural exhibits from the nation's first people.

British Columbia Forest Discovery Centre

View interactive exhibits from the forestry industry in British Columbia.
