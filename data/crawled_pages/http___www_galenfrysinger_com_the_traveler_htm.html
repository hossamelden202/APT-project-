I am a retired scientist (chemistry and physics) who now spends most of his time traveling to interesting places in the world. After getting my PhD from Yale I spent time doing research in Germany on Fulbright and National Science Foundation grants, worked in University research, for the Federal government and with industry. Most of my work was with new products.  I have now changed my field to Comparative Ethnography. This gives me a reason to look into all parts of the world, whether it be places that exceed a temperatures of 100 degrees or those that require umbrellas more than 60% of the time.

 

I have traveled to 180 independent countries plus 91 dependencies which are separate enough due to geography, culture or history to warrant being considered separate territories.
