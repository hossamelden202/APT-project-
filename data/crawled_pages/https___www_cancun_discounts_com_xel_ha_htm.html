ATTRACTIONS

 

The biggest attraction at Xel-Ha is the Swim With Dolphins activity.  You can interact with the dolphins including being foot-pushed, and have them do tricks in front of and over you.  The dolphin swim takes place in the lagoon.  The other major thing to see and do here is the lagoon itself.  Xel-Ha is one of the best locations in Cancun for snorkeling.  There is also cliff diving, a rope swing, and a great beach to relax on.  Across the street from the park are some small ruins.

OUR RECOMMENDATIONS


If you really want to see and experience Xel-Ha in all it's magnificent glory, you should opt for the Xel-Ha All-Inclusive tour.  The Xel-Ha Tulum Combo does leave you wishing you had more time there, though it is a good way to get to see the Tulum ruins, especially if you have kids who will get too bored if they have to look at ruins all day long.  A private tour will help you maximize your time at the park.

If you want to do the dolphin swim, make sure you reserve online well in advance.  There are only about 50 slots a day for the thousands of daily visitors to this park.  If you don't reserve in advance, you will likely not get to swim with the dolphins at Xel-Ha.  If you do the swim, there will be videos and photos for you to purchase afterward if you like (we recommend this - a great memory).

HOW TO GET TO XEL-HA:


Xel-Ha is located 76 miles (122km) south of Cancun, just 8 miles (13km) from the Mayan Ruins of Tulum on the east side of the Carraterra Chetumal.  The close proximity makes this an easy combination to visit in a single day.  The vast majority of visitors to Xel-Ha go with a tour like those suggested above.  This is by far the easiest and most convenient way to get there - it even includes hotel pickup and drop-off.

For the independent traveler, you can also rent a car, take a taxi, or catch a collectivo from Cancun.  The Collectivo will let you off the highway by the Xel-Ha sign.  Taking a taxi from Playa Del Carmen will set you back about $30 each way, from Cancun will cost more like $75 each way.  The collectivo will cost just a few dollars but of course is by far the least convenient method of transport.
