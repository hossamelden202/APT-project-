Statistics & Facts

The Washington state capital is Olympia.
The population of Wenatchee is approximately 29,968 (2006), 31925 (2010).
The approximate number of families is 11,486 (2000), 12379 (2010).
The amount of land area in Wenatchee is 15.816 sq. kilometers.
The amount of land area in Wenatchee is 6.88 sq. miles.
The amount of surface water is 1.148 sq kilometers.
The distance from Wenatchee to Washington DC is 2363 miles.
The distance to the Washington state capital is 128 miles. (as the crow flies)
Wenatchee is positioned 47.42 degrees north of the equator and 120.32 degrees west of the prime meridian.
Wenatchee elevation is 780 feet above sea level.
Wenatchee per capita income is $ 19,498 (2000).
Wenatchee median income is $ 35,800 (2005).
The Wenatchee median home price is $ 155,100 (2005).
Wenatchee average annual precipitation is 9 inches peryear.
Wenatchee average annual snowfall is 35 inches per year.

Location

 in central Washington about 59 miles from Yakima and about 129 miles from Seattle. Other nearby communities include West Wenatchee, East Wenatchee, South Wenatchee, East Wenatchee Bench, Sunnyslope, Malaga, Cashmere and Rock Island.

History & History Related Items

Wenatchee is a fairly common name in this area. Others sharing it include the Wenatchee River, Lake Wenatchee, the Wenatchee National Forest and several other communities that use the name. The city was named for the nearby Wenatchi Indian tribe.
