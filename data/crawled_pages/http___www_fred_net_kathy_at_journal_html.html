  • 1995 and 1996 Hikes

    • In February of '96, I received an email from someone who asked if I would be interested in posting an account of his and his wife's planned thru-hike. He was planning to take along a one pound Zaurus computer and could send reports from various towns along the way. I thought that sounded like a great idea, but secretly wondered if I would ever hear anything further. I most certainly did!!! Here, then is the Kushmans' page.

    • The Through-Hiking Vikings have an online book about their 1996 hike.
    • Some time in March '96 or so, I heard from Waldo Jacquith. He was planning to have his own webpage and would be carrying a laptop and cellphone with him on his hike so he could maintain the page directly from the trail. Things didn't exactly go according to plan, and he hasn't yet finished the trail...but he's got some wonderful journal entries.

    • Also in the spring of '96, Pete Fornof began posting Gail Johnson's accounts of her ongoing thru-hike to the AT-L mailing list. Eventually, Gutsy's Journal was compiled into its own page and put online.

    • In September 1997, I found Wired's Page about his 1995 and 1996 hikes.

    • Kevin Hoffman has put up his Snail-No-More Photo Journal of his '96 hike.

    • Benjamin Reese has an A.T. Yearbook for 1996.

    • Bud and Vicki Hogan have put up a page for their 1996 hike from Amacalola Falls to Damascus.

    • In 1995, the Raleigh News and Observer put up a website entitled "An Appalachian Adventure". Reporters and photographers from several different newspapers did a sort of relay thru-hike of the trail, uploading reports at various points along the way when they would be met by support staff with laptops and a ride to a local motel.

    • Also in '95, there was a "Tag-Team" AOL relay hike of the A.T. However, those not on AOL heard little about it.

    • Julius Daniels aka Kickatree attempted his hike in 1996. He had a short but wonderful time considering he had never backpacked before.
    • Mark Holmes has a page about his and his wife's 1995 hike. It includes a page for links to pages of fellow hikers, many from the AT-L mailing list.

    • The Professor has put up a photo journal of his '95 hike. It's chock full of shelters and bridges (as of 1-26-27) with lots more to be added shortly. (Page seems to have disappeared. Please let me know if you find a new URL for his page.)

  • Earlier Accounts

    • In early 1996, I received an email from Richard Kushman asking if I would be interested in receiving email reports from along the trail and putting them on the webpage. I believe The Kushmans' hike was the first thru-hike effort to be posted online via email onto a webpage.
    • Probably the first online A.T. account was that of Gerry Gladu (Moonshot)'s '93 thru-hike. As he hiked northward, he sent postcards to friends who then transcribed them and emailed them to Tim Dalton, who in turn sent them out to a mailing list of friends and also posted them to USENET.
