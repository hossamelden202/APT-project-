BROADMOOR GOLF COURSE
PORTLAND, OR

 

Broadmoor Golf Club is located on Columbia Blvd. in North Portland.  It borders on the West end of the Portland Airport and wanders back and forth across Columbia Slough.  It's quite a lovely course, actually - very serene - you can see Great Blue Herons in the slough as you play.  Broadmoor also honors the Entertainment Book, so you can get unlimited rounds at 20% off if you have one of these great coupon books, giving you a great price for an excellent course.

BROADMOOR GOLF CLUB - OUR COMMENTS

Maintenance:  The overall maintenance of the course is very good, but there were areas on the fairways that could use some tuning up, as you see in the photo above.  Certainly nice to play, though.

Scenery: Beautiful course - lots of water as you cross over creeks, the Columbia Slough, and other waterways.  Nice vistas from the 1st and 10th holes especially.  The only drawback is a couple of holes that border the Airport - kind of cool seeing the planes come in so close, but the noise detracts from the serenity, and it isn't bordered off well by the landscaping.  However, these few holes don't take away from the overall beauty of this course.  We were so relaxed after playing a round here...

Fun: The fairways are mostly straight, but the relief of the ground is constantly changing, and you hit back and forth over the waterways which is fun.  We enjoyed this course very much - quite challenging, actually, and quite interesting.  There are some really nice hills like right off the first tee too - the 9th hole is a nice uphill jaunt.

Safety: The fairways are mostly lined with trees, providing a nice barrier for stray golf balls, and are spaced enough we didn't notice any issue with this.  The course doesn't cross any streets which is nice too.

Staff:  The guys seem nice enough - we didn't interact with them enough to get a good feel, though...

Design: The lay of the land is well played here - lots of terrain, even though the fairways are quite straight.  The landscape is very beautiful, and the many natural features of the course are well integrated.  There were several places we thought they could have integrated the water features more into the holes, however, and we do wish all the fairways weren't so straight.  We like variety.

Value:  For the $9 for 9 holes or $17.50 for 18 holes you'll pay with the Entertainment Book,  this course is a heck of a value for the money. 

Other Comments:  The course is convenient to get to in North Portland - it's location on Columbia allows you to avoid much of the traffic if you're coming in from Vancouver like us.  We really enjoyed that once we went to the back of the clubhouse where the course is, we could never imagine that just a few steps away was the busy industrial district and a major highway.  Quiet, serene, and peaceful - miles away from all that ruckuss.
