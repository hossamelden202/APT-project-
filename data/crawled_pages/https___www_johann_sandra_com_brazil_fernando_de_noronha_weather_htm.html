FERNANDO DE NORONHA CLIMATE & WEATHER


Beautiful Fernando de Noronha, Brazil		 Fernando de Noronha has one of the most consistent climates of any vacation spot in the world.  Temperatures are always in the 80s with warm sunshine on most days, with low temperatures falling only to the mid-70s, creating a wonderful and consistent climate for your vacation.  The dry season is from July through February, where rain is almost non-existent.  Even during the rainy season (March through June), rain normally comes in quick tropical cloudbursts, followed by bright sunshine.

The weather in Fernando de Noronha is free of hurricanes, cyclones, or tsunamis, which makes for a nice, worry-free vacation.  Expect warm sunshine for at least most of your trip any time of  the year, but especially during the summer (dry season).

Important note about the weather forecast:  As with all tropical climates, if you see showers or scattered thunderstorms in the forecast, don't worry.  Even if there will be a quick five-minute shower at night, with the rest of the day being sunny, the little picture in the window will show showers.  It's only if it says constant thunderstorms or constant rain that there is anything to concern yourself about.  Showers in the forecast will not disrupt your vacation.

FERNANDO DE NORONHA WATER TEMPERATURE

The water temperature in Noronha is about 80°F all year round, making for bathtub warm conditions all year.  The visibility ranges from 120 feet in the winter months (April through November) to about 50 feet in the summer months (December to March), so the best snorkeling and diving is done in the winter months (remember winter in Brazil is summer in North America).
