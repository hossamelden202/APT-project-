The Boulevard

(El Cajon Boulevard, that is.)

Over 900 businesses comprise this linear community that runs east from Park Boulevard to 54th Street.

One of San Diego's oldest suburban thoroughfares, many improvements are going on that include landscaped medians, sidewalks with street trees storefront improvements and lighting.

These and more, promise to preserve one of the best places in San Diego to cruise in your convertible, and just hang out with friends.

Farmer's Market -- Every Sunday from 10am to 2pm The Boulevard hosts a farmers' market. Located at the intersection of Marlborough and El Cajon Blvd., you can find fresh flowers, baked goods, organic produce and many fine specialty items.

Lafayette Hotel -- If you are going to stay anywhere in San Diego and visit The Boulevard, do your self a favor and stay at the Lafayette Hotel now run by Inn Suites Hotels. This is a classic in many ways. Book your reservation HERE.

Red Fox Steak House -- Another classic, this steak house and piano bar lounge long ago set the standard in style and atmosphere.

Rudford's Restaurant -- Since 1949 this 24-hour restaurant has served up home-style cooking in a what you want, when you want it sort of way. Not a single chain restaurant/coffee shop even comes close to value you'll find here.
