You want more?  Isn't That Enough?!?

You're sure demanding!  Okay - here's the lowdown on how it works in Vallarta: When you get to Puerto Vallarta, you will have two ways to book tours. One is through the timeshare hawks at the airport or on the streets. Many of these operations are shady, and you don't know what you'll get if anything.  Most cheap tours end with you in a chair getting slammed with a sales pitch for four hours.  Also never give money to a guy on the beach or street who walks up to you and tries to sell you tours.  It has happened many times that these guys are not authorized to sell tours and you find out too late that you have been scammed out of your money.  Some are legitimate, some are not.  Will you know the difference?

The second is through your hotel tour desk.  This is also accompanied by an invitation for a "welcome package" which means timeshare presentation.  If you say no, they often will still try to push you into the tours that they get the best commission on.  You'll also pay more than you will through us.  We offer a discount on every tour or activity in Puerto Vallarta. Q: CAN YOU MAIL MY DISCOUNT CARD TO ME? CAN YOU DELIVER TO MY HOTEL?
A: No can do.  The mail in Mexico is not like it is back home.  You'd probably never see it, or if you did it would be two weeks after you got home.  The hotels won't allow travel agents to enter the property - they're worried you'll try to steal their business.  We can't get past security.  So, we've decided to e-mail the cards.  We think that's the easiest and best way.  Just size them to a normal size, print them out, fold them in half and tape or staple the two sides together.  If you have access to a laminating machine, laminating the cards is an excellent way to preserve them for use throughout your trip.

Q: why don't most of your tours offer hotel pickup? how do I get there?
A: In Puerto Vallarta, we have several different hotel zones that are quite a ways apart from each other. It would take at least an hour or more to pick up everyone at their hotel. Nobody wants to sit on a bus for an hour each way while the bus goes from one hotel to the next.  Not only that, but the Taxi Drivers union won't let buses into the hotels to pick up.  In Puerto Vallarta, the easiest way to get around is by Taxi.  Just tell them where you want to go and they'll take you there.  We'll send you directions to the departure point with your confirmation.  It is a much better use of your time to just meet at one location and go from there. 

Q: how long will it take to take a taxi?  how much will it cost?
A: Taxis to downtown Puerto Vallarta are about $6.00 USD and take about 10 - 15 minutes.  To Nuevo Vallarta, it's also about 10 - 15 minutes, but about $10.00.

Q: IS THERE A BUS LIKE IN CANCUN?
A: Yes, there is a city bus - it is very cheap and easy to ride.  Not quite as straight forward as Cancun, but it goes up and down the highway all day long.

OUR POLICIES

Q: what if MY CRUISE SHIP DOESN'T MAKE PORT?
A: We are proud to feature Puerto Vallarta's only No-Port, No-Pay policy!  If during your sailing, your ship has to reschedule and doesn't make port due to weather, mechanical failure, or other unforeseen circumstances, just give us a call or drop us an e-mail as soon as you find out, and we'll cheerfully refund your money, or change your date if you prefer. How great is that?  We take care of our customers at Puerto Vallarta Tours - the same way you take care of us!  Exceptions: Dolphins, Rhythms of the Night, Las Caletas, Fishing and Boat Charters are subject to our normal cancellation policy.

Q: what if it rains on the day of my tour?
A: It could rain - you are in the tropics.  During the summer months in Puerto Vallarta, it can rain almost every day.  The rest of the year is very dry, so the chances are unlikely. If it does rain on the day of your tour, all the tours still run, so there are no refunds.  However, if there is a storm, and the tour is cancelled, you will receive a full refund.  The rain is usually very short lived, and usually gives way to bright sunshine. The temperatures are still around 90°F (32°C), so the rain is very warm, and it actually feels quite good! There's never a need to cancel a tour in Puerto Vallarta because of the rain.  Please refer to our Terms and Conditions for full details.

Q: WHAT IS YOUR CANCELLATION POLICY?
A: Up until 48 hours prior to the tour, any tour cancellations are eligible for a refund, less a very small 5% processing fee to help cover the credit card fees we're charged to process cancellations.  Within 48 hours of your tour, we are unable to issue you a refund, as we will be unable to obtain one from the tour operator.  As we are unable to get our money back from the tour operator after this time, without this policy, we'd be out of business!  However, if something comes up at the last minute, give us a call.  We'll do our best to get you your money back, or change your date for you, but we can't promise anything.  Of course, if the tour is cancelled by the operator for weather or any other reason, you will receive a 100% refund.   Please see our Terms and Conditions for complete details and our cancellation policies for large groups and private boat charters.

We also feature Puerto Vallarta's only No-Port, No-Pay policy - if your cruise ship doesn't make port for any reason, just give us a call as soon as you find out and we'll cheerfully refund 100% of your money or reschedule you for another day.  Exceptions: Fishing, Dolphins, Las Caletas, Rhythms of the Night. Please refer to our Terms and Conditions for full details.

Q: I TRIED TO CALL YOU, BUT I GOT A STRANGE ANSWERING MACHINE OR NO ANSWER.
A: In Puerto Vallarta, there are heavy rains during hurricane season that often knock the telephones out.  When our phones go out, they often revert to the default message that comes with the phone - "Leave a Message" or something weird like that.  Don't fear - we'll get the message.  If you can't get through on the phone, just send us an e-mail and ask that we call you back.

Q: the phone keeps being busy or nobody answers.
A: If you don't receive an answer at this number, we are likely just on the phone with other customers.  We don't have voice mail or call waiting, unfortunately.  Our phones are packed all day long - Mondays through Thursdays are the busiest days. It is also possible that are our phones may be down - this happens A LOT in Puerto Vallarta when we have storms.  If you don't get through, you can either try back later, or you can shoot us an e-mail containing your phone number and we'll call you back just as soon as we can wrestle the phone from our ear for a moment.  If you get no answer at all, either we're assisting a customer and can't break away to get the phone, or we had to take the dog out - yes, he has to go out sometimes.  In Downtown Puerto Vallarta, you can't let him run free - you have to supervise.  Just try back soon.  We're sorry for any inconvenience.
