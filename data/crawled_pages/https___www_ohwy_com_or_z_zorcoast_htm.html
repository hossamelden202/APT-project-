Oregon Coast

Sandy beaches punctuated by rocky headlands mark the Oregon Coast all the way from its start at the mouth of the Columbia River to the California border south of Brookings. For over three hundred miles, the Oregon Coast presents ever-changing pictures of beauty.

Offshore rocks shelter marine mammals, coastal rivers end in estuaries that are among the biologically richest places on earth. The people of the Oregon Coast enjoy their home and welcome millions of visitors each year to share the opportunities for recreation--fishing, hiking, off-road vehicles, camping, photography, golf, boating, scuba diving, surfing ... The list is endless.
