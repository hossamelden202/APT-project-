Description


Beautiful, Kanahâ Beach Park rests on a peaceful coastal expanse between Kahului Bay and Spreckelsville Beach. Breathtaking views of the West Maui Mountains and ‘?ao Valley await visitors. A swim, a picnic in the shade of the tall trees flanking the beach, or a game of beach volleyball can be enjoyed as well.
 
 Lae’ula O Kai Canoe Club’s headquarters is maintained at the beachfront pavilion, and windsurfers often make good use of Kanahâ’s favorable offshore breezes. Street signs along Amala Place point the way. The beach is within walking distance of Kahului Airport and easily accessible from Hâna Highway 36 with ample parking available.

Amenities

Provisions Information
Park size 40 acres of broad white sand beach surrounded by trees
Park structures Canoe halau (covered, open-walled, barn-style; has permanent stage)
Swimming No lifeguard on duty; Station manned 8:00 am - 5:00 pm
Picnic areas 13 grills, 23 tables, and centrally located water taps by the beach edge / tree line
Sports Volleyball (3 sand courts with poles; no nets or balls provided)
Activities Windsurfing, kite surfing, camping, mountain biking, canoeing 
Parking 4 lots with 189 spaces (Lot #1: 55 spaces, Lot #2: 21 spaces, Lot #3: 15 spaces, Lot #4: 98 spaces)

All lots have ADA-reserved spaces; Lots are numbered sequentially from the westernmost; Each park entrance leads to a lot
Public Restrooms 2 pavilions (adjacent to lifeguard office and near Parking Lot 1)
Water Outdoor showers, water taps, water fountains
ADA Accessibility ADA parking, restrooms at Parking Lot #1
Electrical No public use; No overhead or other lighting provided at the park
Offices Lifeguard office attached to garage; Ocean rescue equipment storage
Emergency Immediate support from lifeguard staff (8:00 am - 4:30 pm)
