Getting To Victoria B.C.

Save 70% on Cruises to Victoria and Alaska!
 The best rates on the web for your next cruise!

Most visitors to Victoria arrive by ferry (including us) either from Vancouver or Seattle.  There are also ferries from other islands, and Bellingham and Port Angeles, Washington.  Other people arrive by float plane or jet into Victoria International Airport.  Still others arrive via cruise ship.

Make sure you bring your passport or other proof of citizenship when entering Canada.  Your U.S. driver's license will NOT be enough (thanks to Sept. 11)!

Discounted travel reservations aren't too hard to find.  We offer up to 70% off your travel reservations at our online store.  Airlines such as Alaska Airlines also offer discounted package deals to Victoria.  The Vancouver Island Entertainment Book offers airline and ferry discounts as well, up to 50%.

Automobile Ferry Service

B.C. Ferries

With 36 vessels visiting 46 different destinations, B.C. Ferries is the biggest ferry provider in British Columbia.  The ferries leave from Tsawassen, just south of Vancouver, and arrive in Swartz Bay, twenty minutes north of Victoria.  Trips take around 90 minutes.  If you want to bring your car from Vancouver, this is the ferry to take.  Click the links for driving directions, fares, and schedules.

M.V. Coho

The option for those on the peninsula side of Puget Sound, the Coho departs from Port Angeles up to four times daily.

Washington State Ferries

One daily round-trip from Anacortes to Sidney during the spring and fall, and two daily round-trips during the summer months.

Passenger Only Ferries

Victoria Clipper

Departing from Seattle, these high-speed ferries offer from one to four departures daily, depending on the season.  Click for fares and schedules.

Victoria-San Juan Cruises

These guys offer one round trip daily during the summer. Click for fares and schedules.

Victoria Express

Offering twice-daily departures from Port Angeles to Victoria's Inner Harbor.  Click for fares and schedules.

Arrival by Air

Victoria International Airport isn't very big, and most International Airlines don't fly here.  Smaller outfits like Horizon Air service it, though.  To find the best airfares available, click here.  The following carriers service Victoria International Airport.

  • Air Canada/Jazz/ZIP

  • Airspeed Aviation

  • Alaska Airlines/Horizon Air

  • Jetsgo

  • North Vancouver Air

  • Pacific Coastal Airlines

  • WestJet

Those of you flying in from further away, will fly into Vancouver International Airport, serviced by all the major airlines, and take the ferry or seaplane over to Victoria.  For the best available airfares to Vancouver, click here.  The following companies offer seaplane or helicopter service to Victoria.

  • Harbour Air

  • Helijet Airways

  • Kenmore Air

  • Northwest Seaplanes

  • West Coast Air
