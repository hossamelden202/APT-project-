Statistics & Facts

The Washington state capital is Olympia.
The population of Cashmere is approximately 2,714 (2000), 3063 (2010).
The approximate number of families is 1,057 (1990), 1118 (2010).
The amount of land area in Cashmere is 1.982 sq. kilometers.
The amount of surface water is 0.045 sq kilometers.
The distance from Cashmere to Washington DC is 2372 miles.
The distance to the Washington state capital is 123 miles. (as the crow flies)
Cashmere is positioned 47.51 degrees north of the equator and 120.46 degrees west of the prime meridian.
Cashmere elevation is 895 feet above sea level.
Cashmere average annual precipitation is 13 inches peryear.

History & History Related Items

A Catholic mission was established here in 1873, from which the town began. The settlement was known as "Mission" for obvious reasons. Later, in 1904, it was found there was another community in Washington with the name of Mission, so the name of Cashmere was suggested by James H. Chase because the beautiful scenery here was like the famous Indian "Vale of Kashmir."
