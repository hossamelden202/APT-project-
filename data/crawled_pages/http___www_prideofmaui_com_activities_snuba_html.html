SNUBA Equipment

SNUBAÛ is a
hybrid of
snorkeling
and scuba
diving

Harness, mask, fins and belt
Participants wear a lightweight, highly adjustable, quick-release harness from which the air-line is attached. The harness tow point is located between the shoulder blades to provide easy towing of the SNUBA Raft. Each participant is fitted with a mask and fins, the same as used for snorkeling, including the lesser powered fins to reduce foot cramping and slow controlled movements.

Guests also wear a weight belt, comfortably adjusted around the waist. Certified SNUBA Guides determine the proper amount of weight so that each guest is neutrally buoyant in the water. Participants can choose not to wear a weight belt if he or she prefers to hold onto the raft and power snorkel (continuing to breathe through a regulator) at the surface.

Air Delivery
SNUBA participants breathe through a standard SCUBA regulator system and mouthpiece. Air is delivered through a positively buoyant 20-foot air-line that connects via manifold distribution to the compressed air cylinders in the SNUBA Raft on the surfaceparticipants do not have an air cylinder strapped to their back.

The air-line allows guests to dive at whatever depth is most comfortable, from the surface power snorkeling to 2 feet 10 feet or the maximum depth of 20 feet. Guests are also trained to use the air-line for hand- over-hand controlled ascents and descents and to maintain their vertical control over fragile coral reefs.

The SNUBA Raft
The SNUBA Raft is made from the highest quality inflatable fabric, fully welded seams and shaped for easy towing. The Standard SNUBA Rafts hold 1 air cylinder in a recessed compartment, and the SNUBA Quad Rafts hold 2 air cylinders.

The recessed compartments are designed to allow easy access to the SNUBA Guide to make convenient cylinder changes between tours and to keep the first-stage regulator dry while changing out tanks in the water.

The compartments sit below the water line, providing a low center of gravity and stability to the SNUBA Raft. Rafts are inflated using a power inflator nozzle which is easily connected to a compressed air cylinder. A Standard Raft can accommodate 2 participants, while a Quad provides air for 4 guests.

At the bow, the Raft flies either the U.S. or International diver down flag to alert individuals at the surface that there are divers below the water. Additionally, the SNUBA Raft is never more than 20 feet from a SNUBA participant and can be held onto for support while guests are at the surface.

Restrictions
Your SNUBA instructor reserves the right to determine whether or not you may participate in the SNUBA dive based on your comprehension and level of comfort in the water.

Frequently Asked Questions

SNUBAÛ is the safest and easiest way to experience the exhilaration of breathing underwater. It lasts for about 20 minutes and you will be under the care of our expert instructors. A cross between snorkeling and scuba diving, SNUBAÛ is easy for anyone to enjoy. The air tank is attached to a raft floating at the surface above you, so you can relax and breathe without worrying about all of that heavy equipment.

With Pride of Maui, SNUBAÛ is offered during morning snorkel tours. The duration of a SNUBAÛ experience is generally 20-25 minutes, depending on the guest. SNUBAÛ is an activity available per snorkel location as an add-on to a regular snorkel booking (per person). Contact our reservations department at 808-242-0955 for more information about SNUBAÛ with Pride of Maui.

You will wear a pair of fins, a mask, a weight belt (to neutralize your buoyancy in the water), and a SNUBAÛ harness that connects your air line to the raft on the surface.

On a Pride of Maui SNUBAÛ adventure, guests can dive to a depth of 15 feet. The SNUBAÛ tank is attached to a raft on the ocean surface and connected to a long hose that always remains with you.

SNUBAÛ has a lot of the upsides of snorkeling and scuba diving, without most of the downsides. The most common cause of ear pain from swimming or snorkeling occurs because the ears have not been equalized before entering the water. To equalize your ears, block your nostrils and blow to open your ears (or pop your ears). This is the same action you might take on a flight right before the plane lands. If you have concerns about ear pain from being underwater, please make sure to check with your general doctor before booking any ocean activity.

There are only a few requirements for SNUBAÛ. Guests must be at least eight years of age, have no medical contraindications, and have basic swimming skills. Although we provide flotation devices for SNUBAÛ, all guests should make sure that they are comfortable swimming in the ocean.

Theres a minimal chance that a person could get the bends, also known as decompression sickness (DCS), from SNUBAÛ diving. Unlike scuba, guests cannot reach a depth further than 15 feet with SNUBAÛ. As a general recommendation, guests should refrain from taking helicopter tours, driving to Haleakala, or flying on an airplane for 4-6 hours before or after SNUBAÛ.
