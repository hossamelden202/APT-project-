What to Do in Cozumel

What is there to do in Cozumel?

Nightlife

We didn't do too much partying in Cozumel, since we always had to get up early to go diving, however we did notice a few spots worth mentioning.  In addition to the spots we found, Neptuno and Scaramouche are major discos, and Platino's offers exotic dancers in a night club atmosphere. We also went to another night club with really bad music on New Year's Eve, but we can't remember the name of it!

Carlos N Charlie's

 This was a wild and crazy place.  It reminded us of El Squid Roe in Cabo San Lucas.  There was much drinking, dancing, and all-around partying.  We only stayed to have a beer and check out what they had going on for New Year's Eve.  They had a Millennium package for $100 which included open bar, dinner, and a souvenir package, but we opted not to.  The crowd was primarily college-aged kids, although there were older people there too.  If you want to get really drunk and have lots of fun doing it, this would be the spot.

Fat Tuesday

 This place was an outdoor bar with a covered roof.  The drinks were huge, we saw, and they played really good music.  Its located right on the North side of the plaza.  On New Year's Eve, they set up big speakers outside, and everyone danced, drank and partied.  It was a nice atmosphere they set up, we thought, and we joined in!  We saw most of the people we had been diving with there too!  The place was packed, and everyone had fun until such morning hours as we didn't survive to see.

Shopping - We didn't do much shopping this time around, but there is the usual flea market, where you can haggle with the merchants, and other shops selling touristy items.  We were amazed at the amount of jewelry stores!  It seemed like they were everywhere!

Diving - This is the principal reason for coming to Cozumel.  Absolutely beautiful.  We chose Dive With Martin out of the over 100 dive shops on the island for our trip.  Visit our Cozumel Diving page for the scoop on how it went and what we saw.

Snorkeling - Excellent snorkeling outside our hotel, the Coral Princess Club.  Also good at Chankanaub Ecological Park, and outside most of the hotels.  Beautiful, clear water.  Check out our snorkeling page to find out how it went and what we saw.

Water Sports - You can surf on the east side of the island, and there are wave runners for rent at most hotels.  Water skiing, parasailing, windsurfing, sailing, and deep-sea fishing are all popular here. 

Tours - A host of tour operators offer snorkeling tours, jungle tours, city tours, island tours, and Mayan ruins tours.  The island is so small, that we just rented a car and drove around the island.  The East side of the island is uninhabited and just beautiful.
