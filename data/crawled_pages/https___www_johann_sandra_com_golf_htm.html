Golf

Golf is one of those games that you have to commit yourself to learning in order to be any good.  We play as often as possible, but unfortunately, it's not nearly enough to be what we'd consider decent.  Right now, we get out a few times a year, usually frequenting the local Portland and Vancouver, WA area golf courses, but occasionally we'll take a little side trip for the links.

We've written up some course reviews of the courses we've played, complete with photos, descriptions, maps, directions, rates, coupons and discounts.  More to come soon, but it's all we've put together so far.  To visit a particular course review page, just click on the link below.

On Christmas Day, 2001 we trotted back onto the course for a round at Kukulolono Public Golf Course in Kalaheo, Hawaii, on the island of Kauai.  The course was set on a mountaintop, with panoramic views of the ocean, valleys and surrounding mountain ranges.  Set amongst the palms and Japanese gardens, with roosters joining us on each tee, it was an extraordinary experience.  The course itself isn't maintained that well, and isn't anything special, but the setting, as well as the price ($7/person for 18 holes, $6 cart rental!) made it perfect.  We quickly found out that golf is a game you have to stay in practice to score well at, but we had a great time.

Course reviews coming soon include the Kukulolono Golf Course in Kalaheo, Hawaii, Hartwood Golf Course in Battle Ground, Washington and the Running Y Resort Course in Klamath Falls, Oregon.
