Victoria Sightseeing and Tours

Save on All Types of Tours and Activities!
 Find discounts for all types of activities! Reserve online before you go and save time and money!

During our latest visit, we took a ride in a horse-drawn carriage from Tally-Ho Sightseeing.  The horses were parked right by our condo, so we hopped in.  The tour went by the Parliament building, Beacon Hill Park, the Emily Carr house, and through some old Victorian neighborhoods.  Not the most exhilarating tour out there, I'm sure, but it was relaxing and Sandra's family had a great time basking in the sunshine.

After the tour, we drove along the scenic drive and took in the majesty of the Vancouver Island coastline.  The Olympic Mountains framed the horizon and the mansions hung from the cliffs.  People ran and walked their dogs along the walking paths, and kids played in the parks.  It only took an hour or so, and it was quite striking.  To learn more about the scenic drive, ask the visitor's center.

There is a lot to see in Victoria, and you may need some help to take it all in.  You can take a tour in a double-decker bus or a horse-drawn carriage, or drive yourself around the scenic Vancouver Island coast.  The listings below will help give you some ideas.  For other things to see, visit Victoria Attractions. For more adventurous or active tours, visit Victoria Whale Watching and Adventure Tours.

Also, don't forget to pick up a copy of the Vancouver Island Entertainment Book, which can save you big money on tours, attractions, recreation, hotels, and dining!
