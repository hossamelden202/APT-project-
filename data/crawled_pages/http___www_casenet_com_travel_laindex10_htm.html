Hollywood
Hollywood 
Hollywood Blvd. 
Hollywood California 

Stars in the 1 mile long Walk of Fame on Hollywood Blvd. between Grower Street and La  Brea Avenue. Hand prints in the cement at 1927 Mann's Chinese Theatre which is at 6925 Hollywood Blvd. The Hollyweird come out at night. Lots of club action west on Hollywood Blvd. in the evenings. The trendy set might go to Melrose Avenue for coffee.

J. Paul Getty Museum
J. Paul Getty Museum 
The Getty Center 
1200 Getty Center Drive 
Los Angeles, CA 90049 
(310) 440-7300 
J. Paul Getty Museum Official Web Site

Much more than just a museum. It offers a wide range of public programs, including lectures, classes, films, and performances. The J. Paul Getty Museum houses the collections of European paintings, drawings, sculpture, illuminated manuscripts, and decorative arts, and European and American photographs. At the top of the hills prominentely overlooking the entire Los Angeles basin just off the 405 Freeway. Newly opened in December 1997. Make reservations far in advance.
Long Beach Aquarium of the Pacific
Long Beach Aquarium of the Pacific 
100 Aquarium Way 
Long Beach California 
562-590-3100 
Long Beach Aquarium Official Web Site

Spectacular new attraction for summer of 1998 featuring dozens of exhibits and habitats with 10,000 ocean denizens representing more than 550 species from three major regions of the Pacific Ocean. The Long Beach Aquarium of the Pacific is one of the largest aquariums in the United States. Long lines since it just opened. Arrive early.
Third Street Promenade
Third Street Promenade 
Third Street between Broadway and Wilshire Blvd. 
Santa Monica California 
Third Street Promenade Official Web Site

European-style walk street that offers numerous shopping, dining, and entertainment opportunities. This three block walkway is busy during the day and most evenings. Try Friday evenings, Saturday evenings or Sunday afternoons early evenings. Very popular.
Universal Citywalk
Universal Citywalk 
Hollywood Freeway (US 101) at Lankershim Blvd. 
Universal City California 
Universal Official Web Site

A pedestrian walk with over the top shops that draws large crowds of tourists and locals. Connects to Universal Studio but the Citywalk is free. Well, after you pay for parking. Use your parking stub to get a discount at the large movie theatre complex. Enjoy a bite to eat at the Hard Rock Cafe.

Venice Beach
Venice Beach 
Ocean Front Walk 
From Windward to Santa Monica Pier 
Venice California 

$10 million facelift for this well known attraction. The rededication ceremony took place on Monday 15 January 2001. The renovated boardwalk includes a skate dance area, children's playground, new basketball courts, legally sanctioned graffiti walls, artwork, a fountain, street lights and landscaping.

Artisan booths, street performers, sunglass and T-shirts, hard body weight lifters, bikini roller bladers, bicyclists. Rent a bicycle or roller blades at the rental shops on Windward. Enjoy breakfast at the Sidewalk Cafe just 1/2 block north of Windward. Great place to people watch. Dress real casual. This is a bathing suit and shorts crowd during the warm summer days. The police are on bicycles and horses here. Most crowded during the day from noon to 3:00 pm on weekends. Don't linger late in the afternoon as the sun goes down. Everyone leaves the area before it gets dark. Move on up to Third Street Promenade. Venice Beach is second only to Disneyland in popularity.
